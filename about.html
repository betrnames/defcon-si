Understanding DEFCON.si

How military readiness levels work, what OSINT is, and how we score the parallel race toward Super Intelligence.

What Does DEFCON Mean?

DEFCON (Defense Readiness Condition) is the U.S. military's five-level system used to communicate the readiness posture of its forces. Lower numbers mean higher readiness. The actual level at any given time is classified and may differ across commands.

5

Fade Out

Normal
Peacetime conditions. Lowest state of readiness.

4

Double Take

Increased Watch
Heightened intelligence gathering and security measures.

3

Round House

Elevated
Force readiness increased above normal peacetime levels.

2

Fast Pace

High Alert
Armed forces ready to deploy and engage in under six hours.

1

Cocked Pistol

Maximum
Imminent or ongoing major military conflict.

DEFCON.si displays an estimated level inferred from open-source signals — not an official government reading.

What is SI Readiness?

SI Readiness is our independent 0–5 composite score tracking the acceleration toward AGI / Super Intelligence, autonomous agent capabilities, AI-driven conflict potential, and loss-of-control risks.

0–1 · Dormant / Baseline

Routine AI news, incremental capability updates, no significant deviation from normal signal volume.

2–3 · Watch / Elevated

Notable capability announcements, autonomous-agent milestones, or corroborated AI-security incidents.

4–5 · Severe / Critical

Rapid, corroborated shifts in capability, deployment, or governance signals suggesting elevated systemic risk.

SI Readiness is independent of the DEFCON estimate. A high SI score does not predict a military readiness change.

What is OSINT?

Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) is intelligence gathered from publicly available sources — news wires, government press releases, social media, flight and maritime trackers, satellite imagery, academic research, and other open data.

This site uses only OSINT to create independent estimates. We do not have access to classified information, insider briefings, or non-public military communications.

  • SRC  Open sources only — no classified feeds.
  • EST  DEFCON level is inferred from observable posture, not announced officially.
  • REV  Assessments are revised as signals are corroborated or retracted.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this the official DEFCON level?

No. Actual U.S. military DEFCON status is classified. DEFCON.si publishes an unofficial estimate based on open-source signals. It is for educational and informational purposes only.

How often is the estimate updated?

Updates depend on signal volume and corroboration. The monitor page shows the timestamp of the last assessment. Significant events may trigger revisions; quiet periods may hold the level steady.

Can different commands be at different DEFCON levels?

Yes. DEFCON can vary by geographic command (e.g. STRATCOM, PACOM). Public discourse often references a single number, but the real posture is distributed and classified.

What sources feed the estimate?

News wires, official government releases, diplomatic statements, open flight and maritime tracking, public research on AI capabilities, and other verifiable open data. Each signal card on the monitor shows confidence and sourcing quality.

How is SI Readiness different from DEFCON?

DEFCON tracks estimated military readiness. SI Readiness tracks the AI / Super Intelligence risk composite — capability jumps, autonomous systems, and loss-of-control signals. They are scored in parallel but measure different things.

How accurate are OSINT-based estimates?

OSINT is noisy. Signals can be misread, exaggerated, or deliberately misleading. Machine-assisted scoring helps surface patterns but cannot guarantee accuracy. Treat all readings as estimates, not facts.

Is DEFCON.si affiliated with the U.S. government?

No. DEFCON.si is not affiliated with the Department of Defense, NORAD, or any government agency. "DEFCON" is used descriptively to explain the readiness scale we are estimating.

Should I use this for emergency decisions?

No. This is not an alert system. Do not use DEFCON.si for emergency, safety, financial, or travel decisions. Consult official government and local emergency channels.
